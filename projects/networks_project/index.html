Abstract

The post-COVID era has witnessed a tremendous surge in video conferencing usage, alongside the rapid growth of mobile devices. However, existing methods of video conferencing falls short of delivering high quality video in limited bandwidth. For example, when someone attends a zoom call from a low connectivity space, the video quality often degrades. The recent advancements in the vision domain have led to the emergence of several classes of models for super-resolution, such Convolutional Neural Network (CNN), Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), vision transformers (ViTs), and diffusion models. Existing techniques, such as NEMO, focus on enhancing low-quality video streams for mobile devices. These approaches typically assume that the server has prior knowledge of the video content, enabling it to perform content-aware enhancement and improve the overall Quality of Experience (QoE). However, this assumption does not hold in real-time video conferencing scenarios, where future frames are unknown and inherently unpredictable. Current video conferencing systems primarily rely on adaptive bitrate streaming to cope with limited bandwidth, which often results in significant degradation of visual quality under constrained network conditions. To address this limitation, this project proposes a framework in which a super-resolution module is integrated between the sender and receiver. This module enhances video frames on-the-fly, thereby improving perceived quality even in low-bandwidth scenarios.
