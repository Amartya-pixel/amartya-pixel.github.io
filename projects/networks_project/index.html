Abstract

The post-COVID era has witnessed a tremendous surge in video conferencing usage, alongside the rapid growth of mobile devices. However, existing video conferencing systems often fail to deliver high-quality video under constrained bandwidth conditions. For example, users attending a Zoom call from low-connectivity environments frequently experience severe degradation in visual quality.

Recent advancements in the vision domain have led to the emergence of several classes of super-resolution models, including Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), Vision Transformers (ViTs), and diffusion-based models.

Existing techniques such as NeMO focus on enhancing low-quality video streams for mobile video streaming applications. These approaches generally assume that the server has prior knowledge of future video content, enabling content-aware enhancement and improved Quality of Experience (QoE). However, this assumption does not hold for real-time video conferencing scenarios, where future frames are unknown and inherently unpredictable.

Current video conferencing platforms primarily rely on adaptive bitrate streaming to handle low-bandwidth conditions, often resulting in significant visual degradation. To address this limitation, ZoomiFi introduces a framework in which a super-resolution module is integrated between the sender and receiver to enhance degraded video frames in real time. The proposed framework aims to improve perceptual video quality while maintaining low-latency communication in bandwidth-constrained environments.
